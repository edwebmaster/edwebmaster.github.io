Bob Esposito

Owner & Designer

Bob Esposito was born into a military family at Camp Lejeune, North Carolina. Throughout his childhood his father was stationed in various cities along the eastern seaboard. During that time he was exposed to the rich architectural remnants of colonial America in cities such as Beaufort, South Carolina, Williamsburg, Virginia, and Annapolis, Maryland. Bob’s parents eventually retired to Jacksonville, Florida. It was there that he met his wife and started a family of his own. After apprenticing from 1981 to 1984, Bob began working as a freelance designer until 1994 when he partnered with a local architect to start a residential design company. In 2006 their company merged with a larger national architecture firm. During his time there he worked with the Mississippi Governor's Office recovery and renewal task force to integrate indigenous aesthetics with modular housing for post-Katrina neighborhoods. Bob's involvement in nearly every facet of residential design and planning provides him with a unique perspective and skill set to guide homeowners and builders through the design process. Over the years he has won multiple awards for his practical and aesthetically driven designs. He is a Certified Professional Building Designer and a member of the American Institute of Building Design. Esposito Design is the culmination of Bob's passion—producing high quality, functional, and good-looking home designs.

Joel Esposito

Marketing & 3D Design

Self-proclaimed kitchen geek and computer whiz, Joel helps us develop our 3D design and visualization processes. He recently completed a self-built 450 square foot live/work studio, which doubled as an intensive class on residential construction. He holds a BFA in Computer Art and Design as well as a professional certification in Autodesk Revit Architecture. His unique skill set in 3D modeling and design in conjunction with his exposure to the residential architecture and construction industry helps Esposito Design provide the most cutting edge services in our market. If you ever happen to meet him, ask him how he likes his coffee.

Administrative Assistant
